# LifeStream Vision

## What LifeStream Is

LifeStream is a private, ad-free social network designed exclusively for friends and family.

LifeStream is a chronological stream of life.

Posts may include photos, videos, announcements, stories, polls, life events, comments, reactions, and conversations.

Every feature should make it easier to share life's moments with the people who matter most.

---

## What LifeStream Is Not

LifeStream is not another Facebook.

It is not another Instagram.

It is not another TikTok.

It is not another photo management application.

LifeStream will never include:

- advertising
- sponsored content
- engagement algorithms
- trending feeds
- influencer features
- follower counts
- popularity metrics
- public profiles

LifeStream values relationships over reach.

---

## Why LifeStream Exists

LifeStream exists because sharing life's moments with the people who matter most should be simple, private, and enjoyable.

Today's social networks are built to maximize engagement, collect personal data, and monetize attention. They encourage broadcasting to audiences rather than strengthening relationships. LifeStream rejects that model.

Its purpose is not to help users gain followers, build an audience, or spend more time online. Its purpose is to help people stay connected with the people they already care about.

---

## Design Philosophy

Every design decision should support one or more of these principles.

### Privacy First

User data belongs to its users.

LifeStream will never include advertising, behavioral tracking, data mining, or algorithmic manipulation.

---

### Family First

LifeStream is built for real relationships.

Features should encourage conversation, participation, and shared experiences—not popularity or performance.

---

### Conversation Over Consumption

LifeStream exists to strengthen relationships, not maximize screen time.

The goal is meaningful interaction, not endless scrolling.

---

### Simple by Default

The most common tasks should require as few taps or clicks as possible.

Sharing a photo, posting an announcement, commenting, reacting, or inviting a new family member should feel effortless.

Complexity should remain behind the scenes.

---

### User Control

Users decide:

- whose posts they see,
- who sees their posts,
- which notifications they receive,
- and how they experience LifeStream.

LifeStream provides sensible defaults while allowing users to customize their own experience.

---

### Personal Over Prescriptive

Whenever there are multiple reasonable ways to experience LifeStream, users should be able to choose the approach that works best for them.

LifeStream should provide thoughtful personalization without overwhelming users with unnecessary complexity.

---

### Browser First

Viewing and participating in LifeStream should require nothing more than a modern web browser.

Optional enhancements—such as Web Push notifications, passkeys, or installation as a Progressive Web App—should improve the experience without becoming requirements.

---

### Automation Friendly

Anything a user can do through the interface should also be possible through a documented API.

This enables automation using tools such as iOS Shortcuts, Android automation, scripts, and future integrations.

---

### AI as an Assistant

Artificial intelligence should reduce effort without reducing control.

AI may suggest tags, detect life events, recommend profile updates, or automate repetitive tasks.

AI must never silently modify user data or make decisions on the user's behalf.

Users always remain in control.

---

## Success

LifeStream succeeds when families and friends naturally share more of their lives with one another because doing so is easy, enjoyable, and private.

If LifeStream quietly becomes part of everyday life—and people forget they're using software at all—then it has achieved its purpose.
